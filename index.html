Howdy!

My name is Gregory Allen. I am game programmer from Orlando, Florida with an interest in Gameplay and AI programming. I'm a current graduate student attending the Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy's Interactive Entertainment Master's program at the University of Central Florida. (FIEA/UCF)



About Me

My passion for gaming began when I was around eight years old, as I played my first Pokémon game. I was captivated by the challenge of strategizing team compositions and type matchups in battles, as well as the profound sense of accomplishment that accompanied completing my Pokédex. Throughout the years, I've delved into games spanning a broad spectrum of genres, including Resident Evil, Final Fantasy, Overwatch, and Sly Cooper.

It wasn't until the conclusion of high school that I realized my profound love for gaming could potentially pave the way for a career in game development. I embarked on a journey of learning Unity, C, and C#. As I commenced my studies at the University of Central Florida to pursue an undergraduate degree, I took the definitive step of majoring in Computer Science with the ultimate goal of becoming a game programmer later in life. During my undergraduate studies, I was exposed to a range of subjects including computer architecture, computer security, data structures and algorithms, discrete math, vector math, and functional programming. Though I hadn't allocated considerable time to projects within the gaming sphere due to my intensive course load, I did manage to create some modest games in Unity by following tutorials on YouTube.

By the time my junior year arrived, I had come to the realization that I craved a deeper immersion in the gaming industry. This realization led me to the decision to pursue a master's program in Game Development, as I believed it would be instrumental in facilitating my entry into the industry. For my master's program, I chose to fully commit myself to an intensive game development-focused curriculum. The Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy (FIEA) presented itself as the perfect fit for my aspirations. While I FIEA, I was able to work in teams of multi discplined individuals ranging from artists, to designers, to producers, and project managers. I learned how to rapidly prototype games, work in a large team of 27 people, and how to code in C++. Prior to FIEA, I hadnt had any C++ coding experience but now at the end of the program I learning about virtual functins, templates, passing by reference and by copy, dynamic dispatching, and the differenct design patterns and so much more.

I share all of this to emphasize my unwavering passion for gaming, and to convey my readiness to embark on the journey of creating a game that will capture the hearts of players. I'm eager to dive in and bring to life an experience that resonates deeply with the gaming community.
